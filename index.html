CHECs. For All-

What is CHECs?

Measuring the Usability of Community Buildings with the 'Community Health Environment Checklists'

It is an assessment that objectively measures usability and receptivity of public spaces for people who have disabilities and older adults.

Accessibility describes the physical environment, but

Usability describes how an individual interacts with his or her environment.

A building may be accessible, but is it really usable by all?

Parking/Curb

  • Parking spaces closest to the building are designated accessible by a sign.
  • At least one of the marked accessible spaces measures a total width of 16 feet (parking space and access aisle).
  • Curb cuts are present at all curbs/level changes on the route to the accessible entrance (i.e., at driveways, parking lots, and any other drop-off areas)
  • All curb cuts measure no more than ¬º inch rise onto sidewalk and street.
  • Curb cuts are free of obstacles or barriers (e.g., parked car, leaf pile, newspaper box).

    • Entrance

  • All entrances with stairs have signs posted to indicate location of nearest accessible entrance.
  • At least one entrance is level with pathway (no curbs/steps) OR has a ramp or working lift.
  • The route to the accessible entrance is free of loose gravel, large cracks, debris, and uneven pavement.
  • The route (excluding ramps) is relatively flat (a slope of 1:20 or less).
  • The width of the route is about 36 inches wide.

    • If there's a doorway,

  • There is a clear space at least 18 inches wide next to the handle side of the door. The space is clear of obstacles or debris (e.g., trash cans, ashtrays, etc.)
  • The entrance door can be opened using the strength of only 2 fingers (5 lbs of force or less)
  • The doorway measures at least 32 inches wide.
  • If the door is automatic, The control button for the automatic door is on the latch side of the door (side with handle) OR is otherwise positioned so that the door may open without interference.

    • Drop-off

  • Drop-off areas (cars, Paratransit, or Taxi) are as close as possible to the accessible route to entrance.

    • Pathway

  • The most accessible pathway has a stable, firm, slip-resistant surface AND is free of cracks measuring 1/4 inch or higher.
  • The most accessible pathway is at least 3 feet (36 inches) wide.
  • Objects posted on walls above waist level (27 inches) protrude no more than 4 inches into the pathway, AND headroom is maintained at 80 inches, AND the pathway is free of puddles, snow, dirt, and trash.

    Interior Door

  • There is a clear space at least 18 inches wide next to the handle side of the door. The space is clear of obstacles or debris (e.g., trash cans, ashtrays, etc.)
  • Interior doors can be opened using the strength of only 2 fingers (5 lbs of force or less)
  • The doorway measures at least 32‚Äù inches wide.

    • Restroom

  • Entrance and stall doors can be opened in both directions using the strength of only 2 fingers (5 lbs of force or less) OR all doors needed to access bathroom are automatic and open without interference.
  • Handles/latches on bathroom doors (entrance and stall doors) are located no higher 48 inches from floor
  • Entrance and stall doorways measure at least 32 inches wide.
  • There is at least a 5-foot circular space to access the sink/soap/dryer features.
  • There is a stall measuring at least 36 inches wide and 69 inches deep OR if there are no stalls (i.e., a single restroom) the whole restroom area provides a 5-foot circular space.
  • Grab bars are mounted on two sidewalls close to the toilet.
  • There is clear space under the sink for knee clearance (i.e., not a vanity style) and the bottom of the sink (excluding pipes) measures at least 29 inches from the floor.
  • Sink rim is no more than 34 inches above the floor and all features (faucets, hand dryers, dispensers) are no more than 54 inches from the floor and can be operated with a closed fist.
  • A family or unisex restroom is provided meeting all of the following features: on a level surface (same level as area of business), doorway is at least 32 inches wide, space is at least 36 inches by 69 inches.
  • All features (pathways, elevators, bathrooms, etc.) of the building are accessible, or there are signs to indicate where accessible alternatives are located.

    • Counter (bars, reception desks, deli/ meat counter..)

  • A portion of all counters measures no more than 36 inches from the floor OR there is space at the side for passing items to customers.
  • Seating arrangements (e.g., waiting room, classroom, sporting venue) allow a person with a mobility device to remain in line with other seats (i.e., not sticking out in the aisle.)
  • Table seating arrangements (e.g., restaurant/picnic areas) provide one or more spaces with removable chairs AND the space measures at least 27 inches high, 19 inches deep, and 30 inches wide.

    • Pathways (halls, aisles, etc.)

  • All public pathways (halls, aisles, etc.) and spaces within the facility are at least 36 inches wide.
  • All halls and aisles have spaces wide enough to allow a person in a mobility device to turn around without going backward: a 5-foot circular space OR a T-shaped space (an intersection of 2 aisles)
  • Public spaces and pathways to access materials and services are clear and unobstructed by chairs, displays, or merchandise.
  • Public spaces and pathways to access materials and services are clear and unobstructed by overcrowding of people.
  • The floor surface is free from clutter or debris.
  • The floor surface is smooth (no bumpy, worn, or loose elements) and carpeting, if present, is low-pile, tightly woven, and securely attached.
